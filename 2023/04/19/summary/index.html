Happiness
Discussed the psychology(サイコロジー) of happiness and explores whether money can truly make people happy. It mentions a study where

psychologists talked to hundreds of people to find out what makes some people happier than others. They found three important personality factors for achieving happiness: satisfaction with oneself and one’s life, optimism and a positive attitude, and good relationships with others.
Happy people are satisfied with themselves and their lives. They may not like everything about their lives, but they focus on the things they are satisfied with rather than the changes they want to make. This feeling of happiness comes from within.
Unhappy people, on the other hand, are often dissatisfied with themselves. They feel that something must change for them to be happy and are always looking for something outside themselves to make them happy. However, no matter what they get, they remain dissatisfied.
Happy people are also optimistic and have a positive attitude. They look at the positive side of life and assume that things will improve when they have problems. Unhappy people, on the other hand, are not optimistic and focus on the negative. When they have a problem, they think about how bad everything is and assume that it will get worse.
Finally, happy people have good relationships with others. Studies show that close loving relationships are one of the most important factors in achieving happiness. Unhappy people may not have as many close relationships and may be lonely and unhappy.

Critical Period
This lecture discussing the critical period in language learning. The lecturer explains that the critical period is a theory that explain why children have an easier time learning languages than adults. This idea comes from studies of animals, where there is a specific time when an animal can learn a new skill.
For example, songbirds and cats, who must learn to sing and see respectively during their critical period and if they had not good to learn about those skills, they will lost the chance to learn it again. The lecturer then discusses the critical period for language learning in humans. While adults can learn new languages, it is more difficult for them to speak with a native accent. This suggests that there may be a critical period for learning to speak with a native accent.
The lecture concludes by stating that both animals and humans have critical periods when they can learn certain things. And the human who passed critical periods are harder to learn new language natively, even they are able to learn English well.

GM Food
The lecture about genetically modified food (GM Food) discussed the benefits and risks of the food that added some additional features. Some benefits include: GM plants may need fewer pesticides than normal plants. For example, there is a type of corn that is bad for insects. When the insects eat the corn plant, they die. However, the corn doesn’t hurt people. This type of corn is beneficial because farmers use fewer pesticides to grow the corn and so there is less pollution in the environment.
Another benefit of genetically modified plants is that they may grow better than normal plants. One example is a type of genetically modified strawberry that can grow in cold weather. These are better than normal strawberries because farmers can plant the strawberries earlier in the spring and later in the fall when normal strawberries usually die.
Finally, a third benefit is that many genetically modified plants stay fresh longer after they are harvested.
However, there are also risks associated with GM food. These risks include: GM plants may start to dominate other wild or original plants in the environment, they may hurt wild animals and even good insects such as butterfly in the environment, and GM food has possibility of harmful to people who consume it but we do not know about.
